COLLECTA TECH BLOG

Jan 18, 2018

This is the teams first official blog entry. The team is currently in the process of ordering parts and beginning manufacturing. In parallel, the team has begun working on the vision system for the ball detection. The team is excited about the robot development and looking forward to keep you all updated. Stay tuned for our future updates.

Feb 27, 2018

Machining of driving motor mounting plates is started after all the required stock parts and motors are delivered from suppliers. These include motor mounts for four driving motors as well as the front ball collecting motor and the back gate motor. These motors are different and so require different mounting plates.

Mar 05, 2018

The wheels axles are machined. These connect each of the four rubber wheels selected to the driving motors. These are machined on a lathe, drilled on a mill and tapped manually. The axles work by mounting on the slotted driving motor shaft secured in place using two set screws. The smaller diameter end is then press fit into the rubber wheels for torque transmission.

Mar 06, 2018

The chassis made of aluminum extrusions is assembled using t-nuts/bolts and corner brackets. The machined motor mounting plates are then assembled on to required positions.

Mar 08, 2018

The MDF wooden base is assembled as required. This involves the inlet front ramp, hinged to a sloped storage ramp that is boxed by two side blocks. The wooden back gate is a separate component that is then mounted on to the back gate motor to control the release of volleyballs.

Mar 11, 2018

The wooden base, aluminum chassis, wheels, motor mounting plates, motors, battery and the kinect battery are all assembled together to result in a stable initial prototype.

Mar 12, 2018

The ball collection assembly is mounted onto the aluminum frame. This involves attaching an axle with two blocks protuding out to swing and push volleyballs onto the wooden ramp.

Mar 13, 2018

Initial software programming and setup is underway. Required software packages and addons are installed for the kinect camera and electronic boards.

Mar 17, 2018

Electronic integration and electrical wiring is started where all the motors are connected to the corresponding motor controllers. The motor controllers are also connected to the respective arduino pins which is debugged live during the wiring process.

Mar 18, 2018

The wooden base is disassembled, and blue paint is used to coat all the components for a better product look. This is chosen to complement the silver extrusions and red volleyballs.

Mar 19, 2018

A field testing area is setup in one of the student lecture rooms. The tables are oriented in such a manner to create an enclosed arena with a white background for optimal testing conditions. This minimizes environmental interference with the kinect camera and allows for smoother and more efficient debugging.

Mar 20, 2018

Required electronics are securely mounted at the base of the robot for easy access and neat organisation. This involes the motor controllers for all the motors, jettson board, and arduino. The wires are all connected and soldered as required and then neatly secured taking into account efficient routing and minimal stress on the components. Easy access is also considered for the HDMI cable and USB ports.

Mar 21, 2018

The integrated product is tested in the setup environment. The detection capabilities and the frame rate of the kinect is tested. The robot is run several times to ensure that it can successfully search for volleyballs, detect them correctly using the kinect and follow the appropriate path to reach the ball and swiftly collect the volleyball.

Mar 22, 2018

The final product is cleaned, polished and prepared for display at the Mechatronics Engineering Capstone Project Symmposium on March 23rd.
